## Scientific Abstract

Cognitive decline is a major problem in aging societies. Executive function deteriorates with age, affecting daily living. We anticipate that cognitive training will improve executive function in older adults. This proposal aims to examine the effects of a computerized training program on various cognitive outcomes. We will recruit participants and administer training over several weeks. We expect positive results that will have broad implications for healthy aging research and potentially for clinical practice and policy in Israel and beyond.

---

## Scientific Background

Aging is associated with cognitive decline (Smith, 2020). Executive function is particularly vulnerable (Jones et al., 2019). Many older adults experience difficulties with working memory, attention, and cognitive flexibility.

Cognitive training has emerged as a promising intervention. Several studies have shown positive effects (Brown, 2018; Green, 2021). However, results are mixed, with some studies showing no transfer effects (White, 2022).

Our research group has expertise in cognitive aging. We have published several papers in this area. This proposal builds on our previous work and will address remaining questions in the field.

## Research Objectives

The main objective is to examine whether cognitive training improves executive function in older adults.

### Specific Aims

**Aim 1:** To develop and validate a computerized cognitive training program
- We will create training tasks based on existing literature
- The program will target working memory and attention

**Aim 2:** To examine the immediate effects of training on cognitive performance
- Participants will complete cognitive assessments before and after training
- We anticipate significant improvements

**Aim 3:** To investigate long-term maintenance of training effects
- Follow-up assessments will be conducted
- We expect effects to be maintained over time

**Aim 4:** To explore neural correlates of training effects using fMRI
- Brain imaging will provide mechanistic insights
- We will examine changes in prefrontal activation patterns

## Research Plan and Methods

### Participants

We will recruit older adults (age 65+) from the community. Participants will be healthy with no major cognitive impairment. Sample size will be sufficient to detect effects.

### Procedure

Participants will be randomly assigned to training or control groups. The training group will complete computerized exercises over 8 weeks. The control group will receive no intervention.

### Cognitive Assessments

We will use standard cognitive tests to measure executive function:
- Working memory tasks
- Attention tasks
- Cognitive flexibility measures

Assessments will be conducted at baseline, post-training, and follow-up.

### Brain Imaging (Aim 4)

A subset of participants will undergo fMRI scanning. We will use appropriate analysis methods to examine brain activation changes.

### Statistical Analysis

We will use appropriate statistical methods to analyze the data. Comparisons will be made between groups and across time points.
