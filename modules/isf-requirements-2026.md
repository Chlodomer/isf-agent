## Evaluation Criteria

The **only criterion** for evaluation is **scientific excellence** (מצוינות מדעית).

### For Exact Sciences, Life Sciences, Social Sciences (Section 1.2)

Each proposal is evaluated on:

1. **Originality and innovation** (מקוריות המחקר והחדשנות שבו)
2. **Importance and contribution** to scientific knowledge (חשיבות המחקר ותרומתו לידע מדעי)
3. **Appropriateness of methods** to research objectives (התאמת השיטות למטרות המחקר)
4. **Suitability of researcher** to conduct the research (התאמת החוקר לביצוע המחקר)

### For Humanities (Section 1.3)

1. **Importance and contribution** to scientific knowledge
2. **Originality of topic** or **innovation of approach** (if relevant)
3. **Appropriateness of methods** to research objectives
4. **Suitability of researcher** to conduct the research

### Review Process

- Initial screening by professional committee (approximately 20% filtered out)
- Remaining proposals sent for external peer review
- Committee evaluates based on reviewer feedback
- Recommendations go to Academic Management and ISF Council
- Reviewer comments (without scores/rankings) sent with decision letters

## Eligibility Requirements

### Personal Eligibility (Section 2.1)

```yaml
required_qualifications:
  degree: "Ph.D. or M.D."
  employment: "Minimum 50% position"
  institution: "ISF-recognized institution"
  independence: "Authorization, ability, and resources to lead research independently"

eligible_positions:
  - "Senior academic staff (סגל אקדמי בכיר)"
  - "Regular track only (מסלול רגיל בלבד)"
  - "At institutions recognized by CHE (מל״ג) or funded by PBC (ות״ת)"

not_eligible:
  - "ISF Council members during their term"
  - "ISF Management members during their term"
```

### Institutional Eligibility (Section 2.2)

Recognized institutions include:
- Higher education institutions recognized by CHE or funded by PBC
- Hospitals affiliated with higher education institutions
- Research institutes approved by ISF (see ISF website for list)

**Not eligible:** Researchers from commercial/industrial company R&D departments

```yaml
duration:
  minimum: 1 year

    - "Archaeology projects"
    - "Long-term projects (e.g., Bible Dictionary project)"

```

### Submission Limits (Section 3.7-3.9)

```yaml
per_cycle:
  proposals_per_researcher: 1
  programs_per_researcher: 1  # Can only submit to ONE ISF program
  proposals_per_lab: 1

per_proposal:
  maximum_PIs: 4
  PI_role: "PI only (not secondary researcher)"

resubmission:
  allowed: true
  limit: "Once after rejection (unless stated otherwise in rejection letter)"
  requirement: "Must explain changes in response to reviewer feedback"
```

### Substantive Difference Requirement (Section 3.6)

A proposal must be **substantially different** from:
- Any proposal submitted/funded by ISF
- Any proposal submitted/funded by other agencies in Israel or abroad

**Definition of substantial difference:**
- Different core research question AND/OR
- Different general objective AND
- Different specific aims

If submitting related proposals to different agencies, you must:
- Report this to ISF
- Explain the substantial differences
- Differences must be clear

## Budget Guidelines

### Budget Limits

```yaml
dedicated_equipment:
  maximum: 120000  # NIS
  note: "Include all associated costs (shipping, installation, etc.)"
  overhead: "Not charged on equipment"

student_travel:
  maximum_per_year: 6000  # NIS
  purpose: "Conference attendance or scientific training"
  eligible: "Graduate students and postdocs funded by grant"
  not_eligible: "PIs or other research staff"

miscellaneous:
  maximum: "15% of annual budget"
  includes:
    - "Professional literature"
    - "Publication fees"
    - "Translation and editing"
    - "Photocopying and office supplies"
    - "Internet connection (office/lab only)"
    - "Scientific society memberships (PIs only)"

humanities_books:
  one_time_maximum: 15000  # NIS
  condition: "Books not available in institutional library"

overhead:
  rate: "17%"
  charged_on: "Everything except dedicated equipment and sea days"
```

### Prohibited Budget Items (Section 4.3)

**Cannot include funding for:**

1. Salary or stipend for PIs
2. Salary or stipend for faculty with academic appointment or anyone eligible to submit to ISF
3. Domestic or international travel (except when essential and directly tied to research, or when research subject is abroad)
4. Hosting expenses
5. Payment to foreign guests (including travel and accommodation)
6. Editing, translation, preparation and publication of books/monographs (except under Miscellaneous)
7. Sabbaticals, training programs, seminars
8. Database creation or surveys alone (unless for specific research objectives)
9. Infrastructure construction
10. Storage fees exceeding 7% of purchase value

### Document Structure (Section 6.3.4)

```yaml
scientific_abstract:
  format: "PDF"
  length: "1 page maximum"
  file_size: "1 MB maximum"
  header: "Scientific abstract, PI name, proposal number, title"

research_program:
  format: "PDF"
  total_length: "20 pages maximum"
  file_size: "16 MB maximum"
  components:
    research_plan: "Up to 15 pages"
    bibliography: "Up to 5 pages"
```

### Research Plan Components (15 pages maximum)

The research plan must include:

1. **Scientific Background** (רקע מדעי)
   - Literature review on proposed topic
   - For long-term Humanities projects: describe broader program context

2. **Research Objectives & Expected Significance** (מטרת המחקר וחשיבותו)

3. **Detailed Description of Proposed Research** including:
   - **Working hypothesis** (היפותזת העבודה)
   - **Research design & methods** (תכנית ושיטות עבודה)
     - Include required regulatory approvals
     - Include data management plan if creating databases
   - **Preliminary results** (תוצאות מקדמיות) - if relevant
   - **Available resources** (התנאים העומדים לרשות החוקר)
     - Personnel and infrastructure
     - Access to materials and archives
     - Language proficiency (for Humanities/Social Sciences)
   - **Expected results and pitfalls** (recommended)
   - **Alternative approaches** if primary method fails (recommended)

### Bibliography Requirements (5 pages maximum)

```yaml
formatting:
  line_spacing: "1.0 allowed"

requirements:
  - "Full title for each publication"
  - "Complete citation details"
  - "All author names (for 10+ authors, include main authors)"
  - "Number all items"
  - "Verify all sources exist (non-existent citations may cause rejection)"

starred_bibliography:
  purpose: "Help committee identify relevant reviewers"
  instruction: "Mark with * publications closest to proposal topic (NOT your own publications)"
  submitted_as: "Separate file"
```

## Submission Process

### Step 1: Account Setup
- Create account in ISF online system (if new)
- Verify personal details
- Note: Account creation ≠ proposal registration

### Step 2: Registration
- Register for specific grant program
- Deadline: October 29, 2025, 13:00
- Receive proposal number after registration

### Step 3: Complete Application
- Fill all required fields
- Upload all documents in PDF format
- System checks mandatory fields automatically

### Step 4: Lock and Review
- Lock proposal (Lock the proposal button)
- Generate combined PDF
- Review generated document carefully

### Step 5: Submit
- Confirm proposal (Check & confirm button)
- Submit to Research Authority (Submit the proposal button)
- Research Authority reviews and submits to ISF
